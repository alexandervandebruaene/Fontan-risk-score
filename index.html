Risk-eq. points10-year riskCategory
Research use only — not for routine clinical implementation without external validation. Derived from a retrospective single-centre cohort (Toronto General Hospital; n=355 adults with Fontan circulation). Internally validated via bootstrap optimism correction (C-index 0.757; calibration slope 0.870). Add-on modules are exploratory, use landmark analyses from the date of testing, and must not be combined across modalities. BNP and NT-proBNP are not directly interchangeable; NT-proBNP thresholds are approximate clinical analogues. Total bilirubin conversion: 1 mg/dL ≈ 17.1 µmol/L. Risk formula: 1 − exp[−0.0309 × exp(0.418 × risk-equivalent points)]. CPET conversion: 1.52 + 0.89 × extended score. Catheterization: 3.07 + 0.84 × extended score. CMR-LGE: −0.76 + 1.16 × extended score. Risk categories: Low <10%, Intermediate 10–25%, High ≥25%.

Reference: Alonso-Gonzalez et al. Predicting adverse outcomes in adults with a Fontan circulation: a first-visit risk model with landmark evaluation of add-on investigations. Manuscript in preparation.
